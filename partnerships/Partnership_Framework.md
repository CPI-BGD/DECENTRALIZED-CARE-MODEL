# Partnership Framework for HCV-CARE Program

**Version 3.0 | February 2026**

## Overview

The HCV-CARE program operates through a collaborative partnership model bringing together the strengths of multiple organizations to deliver comprehensive, integrated care for HCV and CVD in Rohingya refugee camps.

## Core Partners

### Community Partners International (CPI)

**Role**: Core Implementer & Program Coordinator

**Responsibilities**:
- Direct implementation in Camps 1W and 04
- Overall program coordination and management
- Staff recruitment, training, and supervision
- Quality assurance and monitoring
- Financial management and donor relations
- Policy coordination with government

**Implementation Areas**: Camps 1W (Blocks A, B, C) and Camp 04 (Blocks D, F)

**Staff**: 84 direct implementation staff across two camps

**Contact**: MOHAMMAD ARIFUL ISLAM, Health Program Manager | healthbgd@cpintl.org

### Rohingya Thematic Monitoring Initiative (RTMI)

## Partnership Principles

### Mutual Respect
All partners respect each other's mandates, expertise, and contributions. Decisions are made collaboratively with consideration for each partner's perspective.

### Transparency
All partners share information openly and honestly. Financial management, data, and decision-making processes are transparent to all partners.

### Equity
All partners contribute according to their capacity and benefit equitably from the partnership. No single partner dominates decision-making.

### Accountability
Each partner is accountable for their assigned responsibilities. Regular reporting and monitoring ensure accountability to each other and to beneficiaries.

### Sustainability
Partnerships are structured to be sustainable beyond the initial implementation period. Capacity building and system strengthening support long-term sustainability.

---

## Conflict Resolution

### Escalation Process

1. **Camp Level**: Issues are first addressed at the camp level between relevant staff
2. **Coordinator Level**: If unresolved, escalated to camp coordinators or partner representatives
3. **Steering Committee**: If still unresolved, brought to the Program Steering Committee
4. **Mediation**: If necessary, external mediation is sought

### Dispute Resolution Principles

- Focus on interests rather than positions
- Seek win-win solutions
- Maintain partnership relationships
- Document agreements in writing
- Regular follow-up to ensure implementation

## Future Directions

### Scale-Up Planning
Partners are jointly planning for scale-up to other camps and settings based on evidence from this program. Scale-up will follow the successful decentralized model.

### Sustainability Planning
Partners are developing sustainability plans to ensure continuity of services beyond the initial implementation period. This includes transition to government funding and integration with national systems.

### Evidence Dissemination
Research findings will be disseminated through peer-reviewed publications, policy briefs, and advocacy to support HCV elimination efforts globally.
