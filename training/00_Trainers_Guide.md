# Trainers' Guide for HCV-CARE Program

**Version 3.0 | February 2026**

## Overview

This guide provides instructions for trainers conducting staff training for the HCV-CARE program. Training is essential for ensuring consistent, high-quality care delivery across all implementation sites.

## Training Objectives

By the end of training, participants will be able to: understand the HCV-CARE program vision and goals; apply screening and diagnostic protocols correctly; provide patient counseling and support; manage HCV treatment according to protocols; screen and manage CVD risk factors; collect and manage data accurately; and provide quality care in a patient-centered manner.

## Training Modules

### Module 1: Program Overview (4 hours)
This module covers the HCV-CARE program vision, goals, and guiding principles; epidemiology of HCV and CVD in Cox's Bazar; evidence for decentralized care; partnership framework; and roles and responsibilities of different cadres.

### Module 2: HCV Screening & Diagnosis (8 hours)
This module covers HCV epidemiology and transmission; screening strategies and methods; RDT procedure and result interpretation; confirmatory testing; patient counseling; and troubleshooting common issues.

### Module 3: HCV Treatment Management (12 hours)
This module covers HCV treatment regimens and eligibility; treatment monitoring; adverse event management; adherence support; and follow-up protocols.

### Module 4: CVD Risk Assessment & Management (8 hours)
This module covers CVD epidemiology and risk factors; screening procedures; risk stratification; management protocols; and lifestyle counseling.

### Module 5: Data Management & Quality Assurance (6 hours)
This module covers data collection forms and procedures; data entry and storage; quality assurance processes; and reporting requirements.

### Module 6: Patient Counseling & Communication (6 hours)
This module covers communication skills; patient-centered care; addressing stigma and discrimination; cultural sensitivity; and handling difficult situations.

## Training Delivery Methods

### Classroom Training
Classroom training uses presentations, discussions, and question-and-answer sessions to convey knowledge. Trainers should use visual aids including slides, videos, and diagrams. Interactive activities such as case discussions and role plays enhance learning.

### Practical Training
Practical training involves hands-on practice with actual equipment and procedures. Participants practice RDT procedures, blood pressure measurement, and data entry. Trainers provide feedback and coaching to ensure competency.

### Mentoring & Supervision
After initial training, ongoing mentoring and supervision support continued learning. Supervisors observe clinical activities, provide feedback, and coach staff on protocol adherence and quality improvement.

## Training Schedule

### CHW Training (2 weeks)
- Week 1: Program overview, HCV screening, patient counseling
- Week 2: Practical training, role plays, supervised practice

### Nurse Training (3 weeks)
- Week 1: Program overview, HCV screening, HCV treatment management
- Week 2: CVD assessment, data management, quality assurance
- Week 3: Practical training, supervised practice, competency assessment

### Medical Officer Training (4 weeks)
- Week 1: Program overview, HCV epidemiology, treatment protocols
- Week 2: CVD management, complex cases, complication management
- Week 3: M&E framework, research protocols, supervision
- Week 4: Practical training, supervised practice, competency assessment

## Training Materials

All training materials including presentations, handouts, videos, and job aids are provided. Trainers should review all materials before training and adapt as needed for local context. Materials are available in Bangla and English.

## Trainer Qualifications

Trainers should have: at least 3 years of clinical experience; training in HCV and/or CVD management; experience in training and adult education; and fluency in Bangla and English. All trainers receive training-of-trainers before conducting staff training.

## Competency Assessment

After training, participants are assessed for competency through: written tests (knowledge); practical demonstrations (skills); and supervised practice (application). Participants must achieve 80%+ on written tests and demonstrate competency in all practical skills before being cleared to provide care.

## Certification

Participants who successfully complete training and pass competency assessments receive a certificate of completion. Certificates are valid for 2 years and require refresher training for renewal.

## Continuing Education

Ongoing continuing education is provided through: quarterly refresher training on protocols; monthly case discussions and problem-solving; annual advanced training on new topics; and access to online learning resources.
