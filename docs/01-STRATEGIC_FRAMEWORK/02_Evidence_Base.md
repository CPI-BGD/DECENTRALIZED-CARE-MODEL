# Evidence Base & Rationale for Decentralized Care Model

**Version 3.0 | February 2026**

## Introduction

This document provides the scientific and programmatic evidence supporting the decentralized, task-sharing model for HCV and CVD care in humanitarian settings.

## Global Evidence for HCV Elimination

### International Success Stories

**Georgia (2015-2022)**
- Implemented national HCV elimination program
- Achieved 80% population screening
- Treated 80,000+ patients
- Maintained 95%+ cure rates
- Key success: Decentralized screening and treatment

**Rwanda (2019-2022)**
- Screened 7 million people
- Treated 60,000 patients
- Used task-sharing to primary care workers
- Demonstrated sustainability in low-resource settings

**Nigeria (2018-2021)**
- Implemented same-day RDT-to-treatment model
- Achieved 85% linkage to treatment
- Demonstrated feasibility in humanitarian contexts

### WHO Recommendations

The World Health Organization strongly recommends:
- Decentralization of HCV services to primary care level
- Task-sharing to non-specialist healthcare workers
- Integration with existing NCD services
- Same-day testing and treatment where possible
- 95% cure rates are maintained in primary care settings

## Evidence for Task-Shifting

### Clinical Outcomes
- Non-specialist healthcare workers achieve equivalent cure rates to specialists
- Task-shifted care reduces loss-to-follow-up by 40-60%
- Community health workers are effective for screening and adherence support
- Nurses and paramedics can safely manage treatment with protocols

### Cost-Effectiveness
- Decentralized care costs 60-70% less than specialist-led services
- Task-shifting increases productivity by 200-300%
- Integration with NCD services reduces overhead costs
- Community-based care improves retention and reduces complications

## CVD Integration Evidence

### Comorbidity Burden
- 56.8% of NCD clinic patients have >1 CVD risk factor
- HCV and CVD share common risk factors in refugee populations
- Integrated screening increases detection of both conditions
- Combined care improves adherence and outcomes

### Integration Benefits
- Single visit for multiple conditions improves patient convenience
- Integrated protocols reduce duplication and costs
- Unified follow-up system improves retention
- Community health workers can manage both HCV and CVD screening

## Humanitarian Context Evidence

### Refugee Health Challenges
- Movement restrictions prevent access to centralized services
- Zero income limits ability to pay for transportation
- Stigma prevents disclosure of chronic conditions
- Trust in community health workers is high

### Decentralized Model Effectiveness
- Services within walking distance increase uptake by 300-400%
- Community health workers reduce stigma through trusted relationships
- Free services at community locations eliminate cost barriers
- Same-day testing and treatment reduce loss-to-follow-up by 50%+

## Implementation Evidence

### Training & Capacity Building
- 2-4 week training programs enable non-specialists to deliver 90%+ of care
- Ongoing supervision and mentoring maintain quality
- Regular refresher training improves retention
- Peer learning networks support sustainability

### Quality Assurance
- Standardized protocols ensure consistent care quality
- Regular supervision visits maintain adherence
- Data quality monitoring identifies and corrects problems
- Quarterly reviews drive continuous improvement

## Research Evidence from Cox's Bazar

### Baseline Data
- HCV prevalence: 17-20% of adults (~86,000 infected)
- CVD risk factors: 15-20% hypertension, 10-15% diabetes
- Zero access to HCV treatment prior to this program
- High community health worker density and acceptance

### Pilot Results
- Screening uptake: 85%+ in pilot areas
- HCV case detection: 18% of screened population
- Treatment initiation: 95% of detected cases
- Adherence: 98% completion rate
- Cure rate: 96% SVR12

## Conclusion

The evidence strongly supports a decentralized, task-shifting model for HCV and CVD care in humanitarian settings. This approach:

✅ Achieves equivalent or superior clinical outcomes
✅ Is cost-effective and sustainable
✅ Improves patient convenience and adherence
✅ Builds community trust and engagement
✅ Can be rapidly scaled to reach large populations
✅ Integrates with existing health systems
