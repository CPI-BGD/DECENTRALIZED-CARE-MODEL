# Target Populations & Program Goals

**Version 3.0 | February 2026**

## Target Population Overview

### Geographic Scope

The HCV-CARE program targets three Rohingya refugee camps in Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh:

| Camp | Blocks | Population | CPI Role | Implementation |
|------|--------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Camp 1W** | A, B, C | ~50,000 | Direct | CPI |
| **Camp 04** | D, F | ~40,000 | Direct | CPI |
| **Camp 05** | All | ~30,000 | Support | RTMI + CPI HSS |
| **TOTAL** | - | ~120,000 | - | - |

### Population Characteristics

**Demographics**
- Majority: Rohingya refugees from Myanmar
- Age range: All ages (focus on adults for HCV, all ages for CVD)
- Gender: ~50% male, ~50% female
- Literacy: 40-50% basic literacy

**Health Status**
- High burden of chronic diseases (HCV, HTN, DM, CVD)
- Limited access to healthcare
- High rates of malnutrition and food insecurity
- Significant mental health burden (trauma, depression)

**Socioeconomic Status**
- Zero formal income (subsistence living)
- Limited access to transportation
- Restricted movement (camp-based)
- High dependence on humanitarian assistance

## HCV Burden & Targets

### Current Burden

- **Total HCV-infected**: ~86,000 adults
- **Prevalence**: 17-20% of adult population
- **Awareness**: <5% aware of their status
- **Treatment access**: 0% prior to this program

### Program Targets

| Target | 2026 Goal | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Population Screened** | 90% (~108,000) | Identify all cases |
| **HCV Cases Detected** | 80% (~68,800) | Maximize treatment |
| **Patients Treated** | 8,400 | Realistic first-year target |
| **Cure Rate (SVR12)** | 95%+ | Ensure treatment efficacy |
| **Morbidity Reduction** | 50% | Prevent complications |

### Screening Strategy

**Phase 1 (Months 1-6)**: Pilot & Scale-up
- Target: 50,000 screened
- Focus: Camp 1W & 04
- Method: Community-based screening

**Phase 2 (Months 7-12)**: Expansion
- Target: 58,000 additional screened
- Focus: Remaining areas + Camp 05
- Method: Integrated community and clinic screening

## CVD Burden & Targets

### Current Burden

- **Hypertension**: 20-25% of screened population
- **Diabetes**: 10-15% of screened population
- **Dyslipidemia**: 30-40% of screened population
- **Obesity**: 15-20% of screened population

### Program Targets

| Target | 2026 Goal | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **HTN Diagnosis** | 20-25% of screened | Identify at-risk individuals |
| **BP Control** | 80% at target | Reduce CVD events |
| **DM Diagnosis** | 10-15% of screened | Identify at-risk individuals |
| **Glucose Control** | 75% at target | Reduce DM complications |

## Vulnerable Populations

### Priority Groups

**Women**
- Often underrepresented in health programs
- Higher stigma around HCV disclosure
- Caregiving responsibilities limit clinic access
- Special training for female CHWs

**Elderly (>60 years)**
- Higher HCV and CVD burden
- Mobility challenges
- Cognitive decline may affect adherence
- Need for simplified protocols

**Children & Adolescents**
- HCV screening for high-risk groups
- CVD risk factor screening
- Health education for prevention
- Age-appropriate counseling

**People with Disabilities**
- Physical accessibility of screening sites
- Modified screening procedures
- Accessible transportation
- Inclusive communication strategies

## Equity Considerations

### Access Equity
- Services within walking distance
- Free services (no cost barriers)
- Multiple screening times (including evenings)
- Community-based locations (familiar spaces)

### Cultural Sensitivity
- Female health workers for female patients
- Culturally appropriate communication
- Respect for religious practices
- Community engagement in planning

### Quality Equity
- Same protocols for all population groups
- Regular monitoring of equity metrics
- Targeted outreach to underserved areas
- Feedback mechanisms for marginalized groups

### Community Mobilization

- **Community Leaders**: 100% engagement
- **Religious Leaders**: 100% endorsement
- **Community Health Committees**: Established in all blocks
- **Patient Support Groups**: Established in all camps

## Sustainability Targets

### Financial Sustainability
- 100% of operational costs covered by CPI/partners
- Transition plan to government funding by 2027
- Cost per patient treated: <$100

### Human Resource Sustainability
- 100% of trained staff retained
- Mentorship programs for new staff
- Career development pathways
- Performance-based incentives

### System Sustainability
- Integration with government health system
- Protocols adopted by Ministry of Health
- Data systems compatible with national systems
- Capacity for scale-up to other camps
