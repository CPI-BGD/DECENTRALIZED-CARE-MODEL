# HCV Screening & Diagnosis Protocol

**Version 3.0 | February 2026**

## Overview

This protocol provides step-by-step guidance for HCV screening and diagnosis in the decentralized care model.

## Screening Strategy

### Target Population

The program targets all adults 18 years and older in the three implementation camps, with particular emphasis on adults over 40 years of age who have higher HCV prevalence. Special attention is given to vulnerable populations including women, elderly individuals, and people with disabilities.

### Screening Methods

**Community-Based Screening** occurs at community health posts, mosques, and community centers with a frequency of 2-3 times per week. Each session lasts approximately 4 hours and targets 50-100 people per session. This approach brings services directly to communities and reduces barriers to access.

**Clinic-Based Screening** takes place at health posts and clinics during regular clinic hours. This integrated approach combines HCV screening with CVD screening and general health services, targeting 20-30 people per day. This method serves patients who visit clinics for other health reasons and provides an opportunity for comprehensive health assessment.

## Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT) Procedure

### Materials Required

The following materials are needed for each RDT: an approved RDT kit, sterile lancet, alcohol swab, gauze, examination gloves, and a biohazard container for safe disposal of sharps and biological waste.

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Patient Preparation (2 minutes)**
Explain the testing procedure to the patient, obtain informed consent, and ensure the patient is seated comfortably. Address any concerns or questions before proceeding.

**Step 2: Finger Prick (1 minute)**
Clean the patient's finger with an alcohol swab and allow it to dry completely. Perform a finger prick with a sterile lancet and collect the blood drop on gauze.

**Step 3: Apply to RDT (1 minute)**
Apply the blood to the test strip according to kit instructions and add the buffer solution as specified. Follow all manufacturer instructions precisely.

**Step 4: Wait for Result (15 minutes)**
Do not move the test strip during the waiting period. Wait the full 15 minutes before reading the result. Do not read results after 30 minutes as this may lead to invalid interpretation.

**Step 5: Record Result (1 minute)**
Record the result in the screening register, provide the result to the patient, and explain the meaning of the result clearly.

### Result Interpretation

A negative result indicates no HCV antibodies were detected, and the patient should receive preventive counseling. A positive result indicates HCV antibodies were detected, and the patient requires confirmatory testing. An invalid result means the test was not performed correctly and must be repeated.

## Confirmatory Testing

### Indications for Confirmatory Testing

Confirmatory testing is indicated for all RDT-positive results and for symptomatic patients with negative RDT results. This ensures accurate diagnosis before treatment decisions are made.

### Testing Algorithm

When an RDT is positive, the patient undergoes HCV RNA testing (viral load). If RNA is negative, this indicates resolved infection and no treatment is needed. If RNA is positive, this indicates chronic infection and the patient proceeds to treatment evaluation.

## APRI Score Calculation

### Formula and Interpretation

The APRI score is calculated as: APRI = (AST/Upper Limit Normal) / Platelet count (10^9/L) × 100

An APRI score below 0.5 indicates F0-F1 (no or minimal fibrosis) and the patient can receive direct-acting antivirals. A score between 0.5-1.5 indicates F1-F2 (mild fibrosis) and direct-acting antivirals are appropriate. A score above 1.5 indicates F2-F4 (significant fibrosis) and specialist consultation is recommended.

## Patient Counseling

### Pre-Test Counseling (5 minutes)

Before testing, explain HCV transmission routes, describe the testing procedure, discuss possible results, address patient concerns and questions, and obtain informed consent. This preparation helps patients understand the test and feel more comfortable.

### Post-Test Counseling (10 minutes)

**If Negative**: Explain that the result means no HCV antibodies were detected, provide prevention advice, discuss risk behaviors to avoid, and recommend repeat testing if the patient has ongoing risk factors.

**If Positive**: Explain that the result indicates HCV antibodies were detected, discuss the next steps including confirmatory testing, address the patient's emotional response with empathy, provide support and resources, and arrange a follow-up appointment for confirmatory testing results.
