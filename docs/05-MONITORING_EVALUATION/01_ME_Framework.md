# Monitoring & Evaluation Framework

**Version 3.0 | February 2026**

## Overview

This framework establishes the monitoring and evaluation system for the HCV-CARE program, ensuring accountability, quality assurance, and evidence generation for program improvement and scale-up.

## M&E Goals

The M&E framework aims to track program progress against targets, ensure quality of care delivery, identify and address implementation challenges, generate evidence for care model effectiveness, support decision-making at all levels, and enable continuous quality improvement.

## Data Collection Methods

### Patient-Level Data

Patient-level data is collected through standardized forms at each encounter including screening, confirmatory testing, treatment initiation, treatment follow-up, and post-treatment monitoring. All forms are pre-numbered and include patient identifiers, dates, and clinical findings.

### Facility-Level Data

Facility-level data includes monthly reports from each health post and clinic summarizing screening volumes, treatment initiations, adverse events, and supply status. These reports are compiled by camp data managers and submitted to the central coordination unit.

### Program-Level Data

Program-level data includes quarterly reports from each camp and annual reports from the central coordination unit. These reports synthesize all patient and facility-level data and provide analysis of progress against targets.

## Data Management

### Data Entry

All data is entered into a secure database using standardized forms and protocols. Data entry is performed by trained data managers using password-protected computers. Regular data quality checks are performed to identify and correct errors.

### Data Storage

All data is stored securely with restricted access to authorized personnel only. Regular backups are performed to prevent data loss. Patient confidentiality is maintained through use of patient identification numbers rather than names.

### Data Analysis

Data is analyzed monthly to track progress against targets. Quarterly analyses include trend analysis and comparison between camps. Annual analyses include comprehensive evaluation of program outcomes and impact.

## Quality Assurance

### Supervision

Regular supervision visits are conducted by camp supervisors (monthly) and central supervisors (quarterly). Supervision includes observation of clinical activities, review of patient records, and discussion of challenges and solutions.

### Training

Ongoing training is provided to all staff including refresher training on protocols (quarterly), training on new procedures or updates (as needed), and training on data management and quality assurance (annually).

### Audit

Regular audits are conducted to assess protocol adherence, data quality, and patient safety. Audits include review of patient records, observation of clinical procedures, and interviews with staff and patients.

## Reporting

### Monthly Reports

Monthly reports from each camp include screening numbers, treatment initiations, adverse events, and data quality metrics. These reports are submitted to the central coordination unit by the 5th of each month.

### Quarterly Reports

Quarterly reports include analysis of KPIs, comparison with targets, identification of challenges, and proposed solutions. These reports are presented to the Technical Working Group for discussion and action.

### Annual Reports

Annual reports provide comprehensive evaluation of program outcomes, impact, and sustainability. These reports are presented to the Program Steering Committee and shared with donors and partners.

## Evaluation

### Process Evaluation

Process evaluation assesses implementation fidelity, identifying what was implemented, how well it was implemented, and what barriers were encountered. This includes assessment of staff training, protocol adherence, and resource availability.

### Outcome Evaluation

Outcome evaluation assesses whether program goals were achieved including screening coverage, treatment coverage, cure rates, and CVD control rates. This includes comparison with baseline and targets.

### Impact Evaluation

Impact evaluation assesses the broader health and social impact of the program including reduction in HCV-related morbidity, improvement in quality of life, and community engagement. This includes qualitative research and community feedback.

## Research & Evidence Generation

### RCT Protocol

An RCT comparing the decentralized care model with standard care is being conducted in partnership with icddr,b. This research will generate evidence on the effectiveness and cost-effectiveness of the model.

### Implementation Research

Implementation research is being conducted to understand barriers and facilitators to successful implementation, identify strategies for scale-up, and generate evidence on sustainability.

### Operational Research

Operational research is being conducted to optimize protocols, improve efficiency, and identify best practices for replication in other settings.
